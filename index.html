Usage Guidelines

  • Keep it minimal: The four greys run the whole UI; cyan is the only pop
  • Accessibility: Night-Slate on Frost-White hits WCAG AA; Steel-Graphite on Frost-White works for small labels
  • Hierarchy: Background: Frost-White → Cloud-Mist for raised surfaces | Content: Steel-Graphite text, Night-Slate titles | Borders: Cloud-Mist at 1px
  • Accent restraint: Limit Spark-Cyan to ~5% of pixels on any screen – keeps it elegant, not toyish

The Circuit Slate Philosophy

This palette emerges directly from computational infrastructure – the deep slate of server towers, the graphite of circuit boards, the mist of cooling systems, and that singular spark of cyan energy that brings it all to life.

Zero fluff, maximum function. Four greys create the entire hierarchy. One cyan accent provides all the energy you need. This restraint forces clarity in design decisions and ensures every pixel serves a purpose.

The result is a system that feels both brutalist and refined – raw computational power expressed through precise, minimal design. It's infrastructure made beautiful through honest expression of function.
