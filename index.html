The Brutalist Engineering Aesthetic

Tungsten (#525252) grounds the palette with industrial strength. As the densest element, it embodies computational density and reliability - drawing attention through weight rather than brightness. This creates a unique visual hierarchy that feels substantial and trustworthy.

Klein Blue (#002FA7) Yves Klein's impossible blue brings artistic precision to brutalist architecture. Used sparingly for links, highlights, and special information, it creates moments of intense focus without overwhelming the industrial foundation. It brings in the "techno" in the techno-brutalist aura we aim to portray.
